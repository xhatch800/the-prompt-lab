# Nature & The Elements
_Focusing on organic growth, weather, and the wild world._

The Root System: Draw where a tree meets the dirt. Focus on the tension of the roots "gripping" the earth.

Fractal Study: Find a leaf. Draw the "skeleton" (the veins) before you draw the rest of the leaf.

The Canopy Overlay: Look straight up. Draw the negative space created by the sky peeking through the leaves.

Decay vs. Growth: Find a rotting log or a dead leaf next to a fresh sprout. Draw them together.

Wind Motion: Try to draw a long blade of grass or a branch using "motion lines" to show which way the wind is blowing it.

Micro-Landscape: Zoom in on a single square inch of moss or dirt. Treat it like a vast mountain range.

The Horizon Contrast: Draw the line where the soft trees meet the hard, flat horizon or a distant building.

Specimen Row: Pick five different types of leaves from the ground and draw them in a neat, scientific row.

The Sun’s Path: Draw the way a shadow falls across a rock. Wait fifteen minutes, then draw the new shadow line in a different color.

The Texture of Bark: Don't draw the whole tree. Draw a small patch of bark, focusing on the deep "valleys" and "peaks" of the wood.

Natural Geometry: Find a spiderweb. Draw it, including the "anchor points" where it attaches to plants.

The Water’s Edge: If you're near water, draw the "distortion"—how the rocks look different underwater vs. above water.

Insect’s Eye View: Get your face as close to the ground as possible. Draw the "forest" of grass blades from that height.

The Stone Pile: Find three stones of different sizes. Stack them and draw the points where they touch.

Dappled Light: Draw the "polka dots" of sunlight hitting the ground under a tree, ignoring the objects themselves.

The Unnatural Guest: Find a piece of literal litter or a man-made object (like a fence post) and draw how nature is growing around it.

Symmetry Check: Find a "symmetrical" flower or leaf. Draw it, but intentionally include all its "imperfections" (bug bites, tears, lopsidedness).

The Distant Layer: Draw the furthest thing you can see (a mountain or distant treeline) using the lightest lines possible.

The Seed: Find a pinecone, acorn, or seed pod. Draw it at 5x its actual size.

Cloud Sculpting: Look at a cloud. Draw its outline, then quickly shade the darkest parts before it changes shape.
