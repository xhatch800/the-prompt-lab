# Spatial Awareness (20 Prompts)
_Focusing on your position in the world and the geometry of your environment._

The Horizon Line: Find the literal eye level in your room and draw everything that sits exactly on that line.

Corner Study: Pick the least interesting corner of the room and make it look architectural.

The Floor Plan: Draw a "map" of your current room from memory, then draw it again while looking at it to see what you missed.

Under the Table: Sit on the floor and draw the underside of a piece of furniture.

Negative Space: Draw the gaps between the legs of a chair rather than the chair itself.

The Window Frame: Draw the view outside, but focus 70% of the detail on the window frame and latch.

Fish-eye Lens: Draw your room as if you were looking through a peephole.

The Ceiling: Draw the light fixture and any molding directly above your head.

Overlapping Planes: Find three objects at different distances and draw how they overlap.

The Doorway: Draw a door that is slightly ajar, focusing on the sliver of the room beyond.

Reflections: Draw what you see in the reflection of a window or a TV screen.

The "Mess" Cluster: Find the most cluttered spot in the room and draw it exactly as it is.

Vanishing Point: Find a long hallway or a bookshelf and draw the lines as they recede.

The Foot View: Draw your own feet and the floor texture they are resting on.

Scale Shift: Draw a tiny object (like a paperclip) as if it were the size of a building in your room.

Top-Down Desk: A flat-lay drawing of everything currently on your desk.

The Mirror: Draw the room as it appears reflected in a mirror (including yourself).

The Backside: Pick an object usually pushed against a wall and draw its back.

Shadow Shapes: Draw the shapes the furniture casts on the floor at this exact time of day.

The Path: Draw a "trail" of objects from where you are sitting to the exit of the room.

# Sensory & Color Filtering (20 Prompts)
_Focusing on specific visual data like hue, light, and tactile feelings._

Monochrome Hunt: Find 10 items of the same color and group them in one drawing.

Texture Rubbing: Don't draw—place your paper over five different surfaces and rub with a pencil.

The Warmest Spot: Draw the object in the room that feels the "warmest" (color-wise or temperature-wise).

High Contrast: Use only a black marker to draw the darkest shadows in the room.

Complementary Pair: Find something orange and something blue; draw them together.

The Metallic Study: Draw something shiny (keys, a spoon) and focus on the "white" highlights.

Fabric Folds: Draw a discarded piece of clothing or a blanket focusing on the shadows in the folds.

Gradient Walk: Find objects that create a color gradient (e.g., light green to dark green) and draw them in order.

Translucency: Draw a glass of water or a plastic bottle and how light bends through it.

The "Rough" Object: Draw the most textured thing you can find (a rug, a brick, a sweater).

Artificial vs. Natural: Draw a plant next to a plastic object and emphasize the difference in surface.

The Color of "Old": Draw something that has faded or changed color over time.

Subtle Grays: Draw a white object on a white background using only soft gray shading.

Neon/Brightest: Find the most saturated, "loudest" color in the room and draw it.

The Sound of Color: Draw an object using colors that "match" the noise level of the room.

Pattern Scan: Find a pattern (wallpaper, shirt, rug) and draw a 2x2 inch section in extreme detail.

Soft vs. Hard: Draw a pillow leaning against a hard wall.

The Glow: Draw a lamp or screen and the way the light "fuzzes" out around the edges.

Dust & Grit: Draw a surface that needs cleaning, focusing on the particles and smudges.

Micro-Focus: Draw a 1-inch square of your own skin (freckles, hair, pores).

# Interaction & Time (20 Prompts)
_Focusing on how things change, how they are used, and your personal history with them._

The 1-Minute Sprint: Draw your hand in 1 minute. Then 30 seconds. Then 10 seconds.

The "Before" Picture: Draw a tidy pile of objects. Mess them up. Draw the "After."

The Daily Tool: Draw the object you have touched the most today (phone, mouse, pen).

The Remnant: Draw the crumbs left on a plate or the liquid at the bottom of a cup.

Blind Contour (No Looking): Draw your own face in a mirror without looking at the paper.

Non-Dominant Hand: Draw your keys using your "wrong" hand.

The Antique: Draw the oldest thing in the room.

The Temporary: Draw something that won't be there tomorrow (a flower, a snack, the weather).

The Mechanical: Draw the inside of something (the battery compartment of a remote).

Human Trace: Draw an indentation on a couch or a bed where someone was recently sitting.

Stacked Time: Draw a pile of books or magazines in the order you last read them.

The "Wait" Drawing: Draw whatever is in front of you while you wait for a kettle to boil or a file to download.

Movement Lines: Draw a pet or a person, but use "ghost lines" to show where they were a second ago.

The Tangled: Draw a knot of cables or power cords.

The Sentimental: Draw an object that has a story, focusing on the scratches that prove its age.

Continuous Line: Draw your entire room without ever lifting your pen from the paper.

The Clock: Draw your watch or clock at three different times during your drawing session.

The "Work in Progress": Draw your drawing supplies exactly as they are laid out right now.

Light Shift: Draw the same window view at midday and again at sunset.

The Future: Draw an object in the room, but add "wear and tear" to show how it might look in 50 years.
